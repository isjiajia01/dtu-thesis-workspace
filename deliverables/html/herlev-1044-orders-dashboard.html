Bottleneck Analysis

Time, not cargo, breaks service.

Baseline cargo use is low, but fleet time is already tight. Under crunch, the system runs out of vehicle-hours before box space.

1. Baseline Pattern
7.3-24.7% cargo used
73.2-82.8% fleet time used
8.2-12.1 stops per trip
2. Crunch Shock
Dec 6-11 crunch window
59% fleet retained
170 → 90 vehicle-hours / day
27 final failed orders
Takeaway: time is the binding resource. Fleet time used rises from 78.9% to 85.8%, and throughput falls to about 93 deliveries/day.

Why this happens

  • Each vehicle is capped at 10 hours per day.
  • Driving between stops costs 15-30 minutes each leg.
  • Service times and AM/PM windows reduce routing flexibility.
  • Lift vans can carry far more colli than they can physically visit in one day.
Conclusion: adding cargo space would not solve this pattern. The operational lever is more vehicle-hours, not more box capacity.
